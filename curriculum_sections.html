Section 4: Competitive Exams – International

United States – Competitive Exams

SAT (Scholastic Assessment Test)
▼

Overview:

The General SAT is used for undergraduate admissions in the U.S. While separate SAT Subject Tests like SAT Chemistry have been discontinued, Chemistry-related passages may appear in science-based essays or evidence-based reading sections.

Why Register:

  • Strengthen critical reasoning using Chemistry-based texts
  • Master interpretation of scientific data and graphs
  • Practice with science-themed comprehension drills
AP Chemistry (Advanced Placement)
▼

Overview:

A college-level Chemistry course for high schoolers. Scoring well can earn students college credits. Topics are in-depth and include equilibrium, thermodynamics, kinetics, bonding, and electrochemistry.

Why Register:

  • Learn advanced concepts through structured topic-wise lessons
  • Access past exam-style MCQs and practice papers
  • Build confidence for college-level Chemistry exams
ACT (American College Testing)
▼

Overview:

A general test for college admission, with a Science Reasoning section. Chemistry is assessed through data interpretation, experimental analysis, and graph-based reasoning.

Why Register:

  • Improve scientific reasoning with Chemistry-based experiments
  • Practice interpreting data tables and graphs effectively
  • Strengthen time-bound reasoning accuracy
International Chemistry Olympiad (IChO – via USNCO)
▼

Overview:

A highly competitive exam where students first qualify nationally through USNCO, and then compete globally via IChO. The questions go far beyond regular school curriculum, demanding advanced conceptual and lab-based thinking.

Why Register:

  • Access Olympiad-level problem-solving practice
  • Master high-order Chemistry concepts beyond school syllabus
  • Sharpen lab-based and theoretical reasoning skills
